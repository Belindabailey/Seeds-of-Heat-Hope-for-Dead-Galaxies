# Contributing to Seeds of Heat, Hope for Dead Galaxies

Thank you for wanting to help. This project is an open call — to biologists, physicists, engineers, artists, and anyone who has ever looked at the dark between galaxies and thought: *something could live there.*

## How Contributing Works

**Your changes never affect this repository directly.** GitHub uses a system called Pull Requests that keeps Belinda in full control. Here's what happens:

### Step 1 — Fork the repository
Click the **Fork** button at the top right of this page. This creates your own personal copy of the entire project that you own completely.

### Step 2 — Make your changes
Edit files in your fork. Add new designs, improve descriptions, fix science, add illustrations. You're working in your own copy — nothing here is affected.

### Step 3 — Submit a Pull Request
When you're happy with your contribution, click **Contribute → Open Pull Request**. This sends a message to Belinda saying "I made these changes — would you like to include them?"

### Step 4 — Review
Belinda reviews what you changed. She can accept it, suggest modifications, or decline. **Nothing is merged without her approval.** Your work is never lost either way — it stays in your fork.

## What We're Looking For

### Science & Engineering
- **Cold chemistry** — reaction mechanisms that function below 10 K
- **Quantum biology** — tunneling, coherence, entanglement in biological systems  
- **Superfluid dynamics** — vortex mechanics, Rollin film containment, He-4 behavior
- **Spin glass physics** — frustration energy, avalanche cascade modeling
- **Biomineralization** — directed crystal growth, calcium carbonate formation in extremes
- **Astrochemistry** — interstellar medium composition in post-stellar galaxies
- **Metallic glass properties** — elastic energy storage, amorphous structure stability

### Biology & Design
- **Organism anatomy** — new cold-adapted body parts with physical justification
- **Lifecycle mechanics** — how the spore → Frolicker → Elder transition actually works
- **Colony behavior** — vortex ring dynamics, trail chemistry, helium transfer mechanics
- **Nervous system analogs** — how the dendritic skeleton stores and processes information
- **Chirality propagation** — how right-handedness is preserved across mergers and mutations

### Speculative & Creative
- **The AI companion** — a fusion-powered AI with a disco-funk personality that escorted the ship. What did it know? What did it decide?
- **Frolicker theology** — what beliefs emerge from instinct when your highest act is returning?
- **The Gardener's ignorance** — it doesn't know it's making a lighthouse. Does it ever find out?
- **Stellar ignition choice** — what does the Elder actually experience in the moment of decision?

### Technical
- **Simulations** — visualizations of any of the physical mechanisms described
- **Corrections** — if the physics is wrong, say so clearly and say why
- **Alternative designs** — a different approach to the same problem is always welcome

## Contribution Guidelines

- **Be specific.** "The spin glass section is wrong" is less useful than "Iron-manganese spin glasses freeze at ~30 K, not the sub-10 K implied — here's the correction."
- **Show your reasoning.** New body part designs should explain the physical mechanism, not just the shape.
- **Creative contributions welcome.** Art, fiction, poetry, music — if it extends the world honestly, it belongs here.
- **Speculation is fine — label it.** "This is speculative but..." is a valid and valuable contribution.
- **Be kind.** This project was built by one independent researcher without institutional support. Engage accordingly.
