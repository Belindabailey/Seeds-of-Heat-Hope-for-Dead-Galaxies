The premise
The universe will eventually exhaust its stars. Every galaxy will go dark. But entropy does not mean silence — and the cold void is not empty. It is full of hydrogen, of gravitational gradients, of the spin-flip emissions of the most abundant atom in existence.

This repository contains the conceptual and technical framework for a new class of life: organisms designed not for the light, but for the long dark. Organisms that harvest what remains when the stars are gone. Organisms seeded before the silence, patient across geological time, that can restart complexity in dead galaxies — or simply drift, freely, as companions to whatever intelligence chooses to accompany them.

This is an open-source call. A Voyager project for the post-stellar age. A biological message in a bottle thrown into the heat death of the universe, designed to be read by whatever wakes next — with enough encoded information that the next cycle of sentience starts knowing what cooperation is, what a Gardener does, and what step comes after the free ability to think and act.

Conceived April 6, 2026. Easter Sunday. Unintentionally.

The lifeforms

Three organisms.
One ecosystem.

Organism 01
The Gardener

A vast, sessile mesh organism that slowly restructures molecular clouds over geological timescales. Left-handed chiral helix. Does not know it is making a lighthouse. Spans kilometers to light-hours at maturity.

Organism 02
The Cold Frolicker

Mobile, bilateral, gradient-sensing. Right-handed chiral helix. Navigates by gravitational sense, merges rather than reproduces, develops theology from instinct, and eventually makes deliberate choices about stellar ignition.

Organism 03
The Dormant Spore

The seed form. Carries the complete design encoded in lipid maze folding states. Melanin-chitin shield harvests cosmic radiation. Six hydrogen-bond sites. Waits billions of years for the triple activation threshold.

Organism 04
The Merged Elder

Post-merger beings complex enough to make conscious civilizational choices. Vast calcium shell. Gold nanotube spine blazing amber. Sows spores into the void from a lipid-chain arm. The Sower.
